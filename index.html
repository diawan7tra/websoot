Maison Margiela

Maison Margiela is a French luxury fashion house founded by Martin Margiela in 1988. Known for its avant-garde designs and deconstructionist approach, the brand challenges conventional fashion with conceptual, innovative pieces that celebrate imperfection and transformation.

Tabi Boots

Tabi boots are instantly recognizable by their split-toe design, which separates the big toe from the others, creating a unique, glove-like fit for the foot. They typically have a slim ankle fit and come in various heights, often featuring a block heel for added comfort and style.

About

The Maison Margiela Tabi Bianchetto Boots are a bold fusion of avant-garde design and cultural heritage. First introduced by Martin Margiela in 1988, the iconic split-toe Tabi draws inspiration from Japanese tabi socks. The Bianchetto version features a hand-painted finish that intentionally wears over time, embodying Margiela’s embrace of deconstruction and imperfection.

History

The Tabi boot traces its origins to traditional Japanese tabi socks, worn since the 15th century for flexibility and practicality. In 1988, Martin Margiela reinterpreted this design for his debut collection, transforming it into an avant-garde boot with a distinctive split-toe. The Tabi became an instant icon, merging Eastern heritage with Western fashion and challenging conventions. Over time, it has evolved into various forms, remaining a signature of Maison Margiela’s deconstructionist philosophy and innovative approach to design.

Materials

The Maison Margiela Tabi Bianchetto Boots are crafted from premium leather with a hand-painted white coating that wears away over time, revealing the underlying material. They feature a durable rubber sole, leather lining for comfort, reflecting the brand’s blend of craftsmanship and deconstructed design.
